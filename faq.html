Answers to common questions about GDrive To Sheet.

What is GDrive To Sheet?

GDrive To Sheet exports Google Drive folder structures into Google Sheets, allowing you to visualize folders, files, hierarchy, storage usage, and newly added items.

Who is this tool for?

This tool is commonly used by:

  • Freelancers
  • Agencies
  • Video editors
  • Marketing teams
  • Consultants
  • Project managers
  • Anyone working with large Google Drive structures

Does this work with Shared Drives?

Yes. The tool supports Shared Drives that your Google account has permission to access.

Can I scan my entire My Drive?

Yes. You can export your complete My Drive structure, subject to Google Apps Script execution limits.

Does this support Google Workspace accounts?

Yes. Both personal Google accounts and Google Workspace accounts are supported.

Does this work with Dropbox, OneDrive, Box, or other storage services?

No. GDrive To Sheet only supports Google Drive URLs.

Does the tool upload my files to external servers?

No. The tool runs entirely within your own Google account using Google Apps Script and Google Sheets.

No file contents are uploaded to external servers.

What information is exported?

The export includes metadata such as:

  • Folder names
  • File names
  • Folder hierarchy
  • File links
  • File sizes (when available)

The actual file contents are not exported.

Can I detect newly added files?

Yes. The tool can compare previous exports and highlight newly discovered files and folders.

Can I identify empty folders?

Yes. Empty folders become immediately visible within the exported hierarchy.

What is the Size Heatmap feature?

The Size Heatmap automatically highlights large files and folders using color intensity, making storage-heavy areas easier to identify.

How large of a Drive can be processed?

The tool is designed to handle both small and very large Drive structures.

  • ~250 items: approximately 5 seconds
  • ~2,000 items: approximately 30 seconds
  • ~15,000 items: approximately 1.5–2 minutes
  • Larger datasets may automatically resume processing

Actual performance depends on Google Apps Script limits and account conditions.

What happens if Google Apps Script reaches its execution limit?

The tool includes an automatic resume system that continues processing large datasets when execution time limits are reached.

Is installation required?

No. The product is delivered as a Google Sheets template with integrated Google Apps Script functionality.

No software installation is required.

What is included in the Free Version?

The Free Version includes:

  • Folder tree export
  • New item detection
  • Size heatmap
  • Grouping features

Export quantity is limited to 50 items.

What is the difference between Personal and Team Editions?

Personal Edition

  • One user
  • Personal license

Team Edition

  • Internal company use
  • Team sharing allowed
  • Agency use allowed

Functionality is identical. The difference is licensing rights.

Do I receive future updates?

Updates may be provided at the creator's discretion.

Major future versions may require separate licensing.

Is this an official Google product?

No. GDrive To Sheet is an independent product and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Google LLC.

Google Drive and Google Sheets are trademarks of Google LLC.
